Simple Derivatives Rules

Power Rule ( Notes )


  • How to solve

    • First off you need to apply the Power Rule.


    • After applying the Power Rule, you can then apply the constant rule to the "+2". Making it "+0".


    • Then you can combine everything together to get f'(x)

      Note: You can break up terms if there is a "+" (adding) or "-" (subtracting) between them. But NOT when you are multiplying or dividing terms.

Trig Rule & Others ( Notes )


  • How to solve

    • First apply the Power Rule.


    • After applying the Power Rule, you can then convert the trig term using the trig rule and combine all the terms together to create f'(x).

      Note: You don't need to apply the Product Rule to "8cos(x)" because it will still create "-8sin(x)". So to save on time and not be annoying, you can just take the term (8 or any other value without a variable like x) and just slap it onto you derivative term.


Complex Derivatives Rules

Product Rule ( Notes )


  • How to solve

    • First identify you f(x) term in the original function.


    • Then take a derivative of the f(x) term you found.


    • After that fund your g(x) term.


    • And take a derivative of your g(x) term.


    • Then combine both terms together using the Product Rule outline.

      Note: You can simplify after doing the Product Rule to make you final answer look good, but isn't nessary.

Quotient Rule ( Notes )


  • How to solve

    • First identify and take of a derivative of a f(x) term within the function.


    • Then identify and take of a derivative of a g(x) term within the function./p>


    • Then combine both terms together using the Quotient Rule outline.

      Note: You can simplify after doing the Quotient Rule to make you final answer look good, but isn't nessary.

Chain Rule ( Notes )


  • How to solve

    • First decide which part of the function is your outer and inner terms. Then take a derivative of the outer term and put the inner term within the outer term. After that take the inner term and finds its derivative, then multiply it outside the outer term.


    • Then combine the and simplify the whole expression to create the derivative.

Implict Different Rule ( Notes )


  • How to solve

    • First take a derivative of each term. Making sure you put along side each non-"x" term.


    • Then move every "x" term to the right of the equation. Leaving every other term on the left.


    • Then factor out on the left side of the equation.


    • After doing that move the everything besides the to the right side of the equation and simplify if needed.
