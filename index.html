Graph Derivatives / Relationships

Graph

As you can see we are able to generate a f'(x) equation from f(x). There is also a relationship between f'(x) (or g(x)) and f(x), f'(x) (or g(x)) represents the slops at different points onn the f(x) function. For instance, the zero points on f'(x) (or g(x)) represents Critical Points, which point on the f(x) function that have a slope of zero. These zero slopes are either min or max values on the f(x) function. So points like (0,0) and (7.385, 0) on f'(x) are all points on where f(x) has a slope of zero.

Additionally, any points above the x-axis are increasing / positive slope values. Likewise, anything below the x-axis is a negative slope value. So from the initial [-∞, -0.397] on the f'(x) (or g'(x)) are all negative slope values and [0.658, 7.385] on the f'(x) (or g'(x)) are all positive slope values.

In physics terms, f(x) would be a postion graph, while f'(x) would be a velocity graph.


Graph

f'(x) and f''(x) is also similar to the first graph relationship. But instead of critical points we have Points of Inflection, which are points that show when the function changes it concavely.

Points like [-∞, -0.2205] on f''(x) are positive, so it showing that the function will be Convave Up (will have a upside down "U" shape on f(x)). While points from [-0.2205, 0.3946] show that the function will Convave Down (will have a "U" shape on f(x))

In physics terms, f'(x) would be a velocity graph, while f''(x) would be a acceleration graph.


Graph Vocabulary & Terms

Critical Points: x intercepts on a f'(x) graph that represents points on the f(x) graph that have a slope of zero. These zero slopes are either min or max values on the f(x) function.

Points of Inflection: x intercepts on a f''(x) graph that represents a change in concavely on the f(x) graph and represents points on the f'(x) that have a slope of zero.

Convave Up: Found by looking at either the f''(x) or different values along the f'(x) graph. Graphs that do convave up will have a "U" shape on f(x) and will be positive values f''(x) graph, will also have a change from negative to positive values on the f'(x) graph.

Convave Down: Found by looking at either the f''(x) or different values along the f'(x) graph. Graphs that do convave down will have a upside-down "U" shape on f(x) and will be negative values f''(x) graph, will also have a change from positive to negative values on the f'(x) graph.
